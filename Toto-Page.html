In January 2013, Toto Wolff made the strategic decision to leave Williams F1 and join Mercedes AMG Petronas Formula One Team as Executive Director. This move would prove to be one of the most significant in Formula 1 history.

Wolff acquired 30% of Mercedes-Benz Grand Prix Ltd, with Niki Lauda holding 10% and the parent company retaining 60%. He took over the coordination of all Mercedes-Benz motorsport activities, a responsibility previously held by Norbert Haug.

"I saw the potential in Mercedes. They had the resources, the engineering excellence, and the determination to succeed. I wanted to be part of building something extraordinary." — Toto Wolff

2018 was a landmark year for Mercedes-Benz motorsport. The company won both F1 titles, the Formula 2 title with George Russell, the Formula 3 European Championship with Mick Schumacher, all three titles in its final season of DTM competition, and both F1 Esports titles.

Wolff's achievements were recognized through the presentation of a John Bolster Award from Jean Todt at the 2018 Autosport Awards. He subsequently received the President's Award from Todt, along with team non-executive chairman Niki Lauda, at the 2018 FIA Prize Giving Gala held in St Petersburg, Russia.

"This recognition means everything. It's not just about winning races - it's about building something that inspires and excites people around the world." — Toto Wolff

In 2020, Mercedes won its record seventh consecutive double world championship. The record was previously held by Ferrari when it won five consecutive double world championships between 2000 and 2004. Mercedes also holds the record of seven consecutive Driver's Championships from 2014 to 2020.

Mercedes' record of seven consecutive Constructor's Championships surpassed that of Ferrari's six consecutive Constructor's Championships set between 1999 and 2004. This achievement marked Wolff's leadership as truly historic.

"Seven consecutive championships - this is beyond anything we could have imagined. It's a testament to the incredible team we've built." — Toto Wolff

In May 2021, Wolff was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Cranfield University for his services to Motorsport. In November 2021, he was appointed to an Associate Fellowship of the Oxford Saïd Business School for two years to transfer his understanding of high-performance culture, team leadership, and personal effectiveness from the racetracks into the classroom.

In February 2022, Wolff's leadership of the Mercedes-AMG Petronas F1 Team was the subject of a Harvard Business School case study, authored by Anita Elberse. Wolff visited Harvard on March 3, 2022, to teach the case study to MBA students and lead a discussion on high-performance leadership and organisational culture.

In May 2022, Wolff was named as an executive fellow at Harvard Business School, a role in which he serves as guest lecturer alongside Professor Anita Elberse.

"It's really difficult to find a team that has won what is effectively a world championship eight times in a row. Usually complacency sets in, or some other factor makes it impossible for the team to perform at the highest level. We tend to see very short life cycles for teams, but Wolff has kept his team at the top for eight years now. I find that really fascinating." — Professor Anita Elberse, Harvard Business School
